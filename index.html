About

The Star Wars saga is a cultural phenomenon that has captivated audiences since the release of the first film, "Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope," in 1977. Created by George Lucas, the franchise spans multiple movies, TV shows, books, and video games, all set in a galaxy far, far away.

The original trilogy, consisting of "A New Hope," "The Empire Strikes Back," and "Return of the Jedi," follows the journey of Luke Skywalker, Princess Leia, and Han Solo as they battle the evil Galactic Empire led by Darth Vader. The prequel trilogy, released between 1999 and 2005, explores the rise of Darth Vader and the fall of the Galactic Republic. The sequel trilogy, which began with "The Force Awakens" in 2015, continues the story with new characters like Rey, Finn, and Poe Dameron, while also bringing back beloved characters from the original trilogy.

In addition to the main saga, standalone films like "Rogue One" and "Solo" provide deeper insights into the Star Wars universe. The franchise also includes animated series such as "The Clone Wars" and "Rebels," which expand on the lore and introduce new characters and storylines.

Star Wars is known for its iconic characters, epic space battles, and the timeless struggle between the light and dark sides of the Force. It has inspired countless fans and has become a cornerstone of modern pop culture.

Star Wars News and Updates

Star Wars Vision Returns with Volume 3 in 2025

At the 2024 Disney APAC Content Showcase, it was exclusively revealed that Star Wars: Visions will debut a third volume in 2025.

The award-winning series first premiered in 2021, with nine shorts by seven different Japanese anime studios celebrating the mythology of Star Wars through unique cultural perspectives. Last year’s second volume took the imaginative series on a global tour, featuring animation from across Japan, India, the United Kingdom, Ireland, Spain, Chile, France, South Africa, South Korea, and the United States.

New Star Wars Celebration Japan 2025 Badge Art - Updated

Star Wars Celebration badges have provided a distinct opportunity to present a broad range of Star Wars characters with a unique artistic vision, a tradition stretching back to the first Celebration in 1999. While the badges are, of course, necessary to enter Star Wars Celebration’s doors, they often become prized keepsakes long after the excitement of new friends, rare moments, and first reveals have receded into memory.

The Inside Story of Star Wars: Skeleton Crew’s Creature Effects

Star Wars: Skeleton Crew, which recently debuted on Disney+, introduces a slew of brand-new characters who have already earned legions of fans. (Neel Nation, we see you.) “For Skeleton Crew there were more characters than we’ve built on any other season before,” says Legacy Effects co-founder J. Alan Scott, whose company fabricated Grogu for The Mandalorian and brought Professor Huyang and Murley the Loth-cat to life for Ahsoka among others. For the latest series, Legacy Effects once again made a mix of never-before-seen creatures and droids crafted by Doug Chiang and his production design team, like Neel and the unforgettable Kh’ymm, as well as more familiar aliens first glimpsed in other much-loved Star Wars stories.
